# Candidates Should Stay Supplementary

## Summary

Candidate notes are a buffer for provisional work memory. They should help recovery, review, and future promotion work, but they should not replace formal wiki pages as the main authority during retrieval.

## Why It Matters

- If candidates become first-class truth, the system slides back toward an uncontrolled archive.
- Entry pages stay useful only when they point first to settled project and insight pages.
- The candidate layer works best as a review queue that catches valuable sparks without polluting the formal wiki too early.

## Evidence

- The [Agent Memory Boundary](../projects/agent-memory-boundary.md) project depends on a clean separation between durable conclusions and provisional notes.
- The [Work Memory](../concepts/work-memory.md) concept excludes raw transcript storage and other unstable residue.
- The original candidate for this rule was promoted only after it became clear that the judgment helped retrieval and maintenance across more than one page.

## Caveats

- In a very small or very new knowledge base, a candidate may be the only written clue about an unresolved topic.
- In that case the agent can inspect it, but it should label the material as tentative and push the workflow toward `promote`, `merge`, or `drop`.
